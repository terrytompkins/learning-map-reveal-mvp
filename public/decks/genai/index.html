Foundation concept

Large Language Models

LLMs are the core engine behind most current generative AI experiences. They predict and compose language-like outputs from learned statistical patterns.

Useful behaviorSummarize, explain, classify, draft, reason, transform, and code.
Important limitThey do not automatically know your private or current context.
Teaching linkThis is why prompting, retrieval, tools, and evaluation matter.

Vertical dive

Why not stop at “the model”?

In production, the model is usually one part of a system: prompts shape behavior, retrieval adds context, tools add action, and evals measure quality.

Human interface

Prompting & instructions

Prompting turns intent into usable model behavior. Good prompts give the model a role, goal, context, constraints, examples, and a clear output format.

Frame the jobWhat should the model do, and for whom?
Constrain the answerWhat format, tone, limits, or rules apply?
IteratePrompting is a design loop, not a one-shot incantation.

Grounding pattern

Retrieval-Augmented Generation

RAG retrieves relevant source material first, then asks the model to answer using that material. It helps when content changes, is private, or needs source traceability.

RetrieveFind relevant chunks, documents, records, or examples.
AugmentAdd retrieved context to the prompt.
GenerateAsk the model to answer using the provided context.

Vertical dive

When a vertical dive is enough

A short RAG explanation may only need a few extra slides: chunking, embeddings, retrieval, and citation behavior. If you need indexing strategy, access control, freshness, and evaluation, it probably deserves its own deck.
